Activities

What can we do to welcome immigrants and refugees to our community? Our agenda is to learn, serve, and live together better, for the good of us all.

Learn. Explore the contemporary immigrant experience by joining a book club to discuss Behold the Dreamers, a PEN/Faulkner award-winning first novel by Imbolo Mbue. Learn about the experience (in Falls Church!) that inspired Mbue to write Behold the Dreamers, in the USA Today review of the book. Join a Welcoming Falls Church book club discussion—sign up to join us on Thursday, May 2—or make it your own personal or book club pick and let us know you're doing so!

Also, join us for an opportunity to learn more about immigration in the U.S. The Pew Research Center, which does fantastic survey research on a host of issues, has designed an "email course" about U.S. immigration that you can learn more about here. This class is free and is available any time, anywhere.

Finally, for anyone interested in going much deeper, American University's Law School is offering a survey course, Introduction to U.S. Immigration Law, see here. This is a semester-length class, delivered mostly online (and of course AU charges tuition). Our understanding is that this course is designed to be accessible to the layperson interested in immigration policy and that you do not need to be a lawyer to take this class. However, you'd need to confirm this yourself!

Please let us know if you are interested in potentially joining a) a discussion group to talk about the Pew course, that would meet in March; and/or b) a small group of people that might work through the AU Law School class together.

Serve. Join us to serve neighbors in need. To be clear, not all immigrants are in need, and immigrants make up a large proportion of the nation's most successful entrepreneurs. Immigrants make up a large proportion of the nation's most successful entrepreneurs. But some—particularly those that are more newly-arrived and face language and cultural gaps—are in particular need. Sign up here to serve our neighbors in one of our monthly visits to the Spend Yourself Food Pantry in Seven Corners, on the third Saturday of each month during 2019. We also invite you to join support 1st and 2nd graders in the community through the Readers Are Leaders program, which is offered by Comunidad, a new and locally-rooted nonprofit serving the Seven Corners and Culmore communities. Volunteer Tuesday and/or Thursday from 5:00-6:00 p.m. to help children improve their reading skills. This may also be a great activity for a responsible middle or high school student with a heart for service—and we would be glad to tell you more about this opportunity and introduce you to Comunidad's founder, Maralee Gutierrez.

Live. Finally, we encourage you to make welcoming immigrants and refugees part of your lifestyle. One way to do this is through the simple act of hospitality. Share a meal with a neighbor that crosses cultures in ways that you might not otherwise experience. Also, consider getting involved in our initiative to organize international-themed potluck dinners that bring together area families. Contact Program Coordinator Katie Adams to learn more about "International Night in the Home," which extends the experience of the very successful International Nights in area schools to homes in the community. You can even follow up on this very locally, in your own neighborhood. Celebrate and enrich the cultural fabric of your own life, by sharing the gift of hospitality and a meal with international neighbors.

We hope you join us in Welcoming Falls Church. Sign up for one or more activities, and if you have not already done so provide us your information in the form below, so that we can be in touch with you proactively about future activities. We look forward to engaging with area residents, civic and business leaders, our schools and many others, to build a community of neighbors.
